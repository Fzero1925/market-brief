Build and maintain your investment portfolio

Asset Allocation

Asset allocation is the process of dividing your investments among different asset categories like stocks, bonds, and cash. Your allocation should reflect your goals, time horizon, and risk tolerance.

The 60/40 Rule

A classic allocation is 60% stocks and 40% bonds. This balances growth potential with stability. Younger investors might prefer 80/20 or 100/0, while those nearing retirement might choose 40/60.

Rebalancing

Over time, market movements will cause your allocation to drift. Rebalancing involves selling overweight assets and buying underweight ones to maintain your target allocation. Do this annually or when allocations drift more than 5%.
